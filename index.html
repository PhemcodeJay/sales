Puppy Nutrition Guide

Proper nutrition is essential for healthy growth. Follow these guidelines (consult your vet for personalized advice):

  • Age 0–8 weeks: Mother's milk or high-quality puppy milk replacer. Transition to puppy food around 3–4 weeks.
  • 8 weeks – 6 months: Feed high-quality large-breed or all-breed puppy formula 3–4 times per day. Look for AAFCO-approved "growth" stage food.
  • 6–12 months: Reduce to 2–3 meals per day. Switch to adult food gradually between 9–18 months depending on breed size.
  • Key nutrients: High protein (25–30%), DHA for brain development, calcium/phosphorus balance for bones, omega-3/6 for coat & skin.
  • Feeding tips: Always provide fresh water. Avoid table scraps, chocolate, grapes, onions, xylitol. Do not overfeed – use feeding chart on food bag.
  • Supplements: Usually not needed if using quality puppy food. Glucosamine/chondroitin may help large breeds after vet approval.

Feed measured amounts — obesity is common in puppies. Transition foods slowly over 7–10 days.
